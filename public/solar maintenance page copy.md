A Solar System That Is Not Performing Is Costing You Money
One of the less obvious risks of owning a solar system is that underperformance is often invisible. Your lights still work, your inverter still shows green, and life carries on normally. But if your panels are dirty, a connection has degraded, a panel has been damaged, or your inverter is running below capacity, your system is producing less energy than it should be. Every unit it falls short is a unit you are buying from the grid at Eskom's current tariff.

For homeowners, this erodes the monthly saving that made the investment worthwhile. For businesses that installed solar specifically to reduce operating costs and eliminate diesel generator spend, an underperforming system means those savings are not materialising as expected. The longer a fault or maintenance issue goes unaddressed, the more it costs in both lost generation and potential damage to other components.

Regular maintenance is the most cost-effective way to protect your system's output and extend its lifespan. And when something does go wrong, getting it diagnosed and repaired quickly limits the financial impact.

Solar Repair Services
We diagnose and repair faults across all parts of a solar installation:

Fault Diagnosis and System Assessment
If your system is not performing as expected and you are not sure why, we start with a full assessment. We check panel output, inverter performance, battery charge and discharge behaviour, wiring connections and earthing. Most faults can be identified and confirmed in a single visit.

Solar Panel Repair and Replacement
Panels can be damaged by hail, falling debris, extreme weather or physical impact. A cracked, chipped or delaminated panel produces less energy and can create safety risks if left in place. We assess damaged panels, confirm whether repair or replacement is more appropriate, and source and fit replacement panels where needed.

Wiring and Connection Repairs
DC wiring on rooftop systems is exposed to years of UV, heat cycling and weather. Connections that were not properly sealed during installation can corrode or work loose over time, causing intermittent faults or gradual output loss. We inspect and repair wiring and connections as part of both fault diagnosis visits and routine maintenance.

Inverter Troubleshooting and Replacement
Inverters are typically the first component in a solar system to require attention. Error codes, unexpected shutdowns, or an inverter that is running but producing less than it should are all signs worth investigating. We troubleshoot inverter faults and carry out replacements where the unit has failed or reached the end of its useful life.

Solar Maintenance Services
Routine maintenance keeps your system producing at full capacity and catches small issues before they become expensive ones. We offer maintenance visits for both residential and commercial solar systems:

Panel Cleaning
Dust, pollen, bird droppings and general grime accumulate on panels over time and reduce the amount of light reaching the solar cells. In areas like the Western Cape where wind-borne dust and pollen are common, panels can lose a measurable percentage of their output without cleaning. We clean panels safely using appropriate methods that do not damage the surface or void manufacturer warranties.

System Performance Check
We review your system's production data, check that output matches what the system should be producing given current conditions, and flag any panels or strings that are underperforming relative to the rest of the array.

Electrical and Connection Inspection
We inspect all accessible wiring, connections, isolators and earthing to confirm they are in good condition and properly secured. This is especially important for systems that are several years old or have been through periods of extreme weather.

Inverter and Battery Health Check
We check inverter logs for error codes or unusual behaviour, confirm battery state of health, and verify that the system is switching correctly between solar, battery and grid depending on the time of day and available generation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should a solar system be serviced?
For most residential systems in the Western Cape, an annual maintenance visit is sufficient. Systems on properties with heavy dust, nearby trees or significant bird activity may benefit from more frequent panel cleaning. Commercial systems that are running harder and have more at stake financially are often serviced twice a year. We can advise on the right schedule for your specific system during the first visit.

My electricity bill has gone up since I got solar. What could be causing this?
This is one of the most common reasons people call us. It usually points to one of a few things: panels that need cleaning and are producing less than they should, a string fault where one or more panels have stopped contributing to the array, an inverter that is not operating at full efficiency, or battery degradation meaning you are drawing more from the grid at night than you were previously. A diagnostic visit will identify the cause.

Can you repair a system that was not installed by EAR Engineering?
Yes. We assess, repair and maintain solar systems regardless of who originally installed them. We will need access to any documentation you have about the system, such as the installation certificate, inverter model and battery specifications, but we can work with what is available.

One of my solar panels looks cracked. Do I need to replace it?
Not always, but it depends on the extent of the damage. A minor crack may not significantly affect output, while a heavily damaged panel can reduce performance and in some cases create a safety risk. We assess damaged panels on site and give you a clear recommendation on whether replacement is necessary.

My inverter is showing an error code. What should I do?
Write down the error code if you can and call us. Inverter error codes vary by brand and model, but most indicate something specific about the nature of the fault. In some cases the issue can be resolved remotely or with a simple reset. In others, a site visit is needed. Either way, do not ignore a persistent error code as it can indicate a developing fault that will worsen over time.

How long does a solar panel last?
Quality solar panels typically carry a 25-year performance guarantee, during which they are warranted to produce at least 80% of their original rated output. With proper maintenance, many panels continue performing well beyond that. The inverter and batteries have shorter lifespans and are more likely to require attention or replacement during the system's life.

Is panel cleaning worth doing?
Yes, particularly in the Western Cape where dust, pollen and bird activity are common. Studies on similar climates show that dirty panels can lose between 5% and 15% of their output compared to clean ones. On a well-sized residential system, that loss adds up to a meaningful amount of energy bought from the grid each year. Annual cleaning at a minimum is a worthwhile investment.
