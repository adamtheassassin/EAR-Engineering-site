Solar Battery Storage Installation in Somerset West, Stellenbosch and the Helderberg
Solar panels generate electricity when the sun is shining. Without battery storage, any power your panels produce that you are not using at that exact moment goes back to the grid or is simply lost. Your home or business then draws from the grid again the moment the sun goes down or cloud cover reduces output, paying Eskom's current tariff for every unit.

Battery storage changes that. Your panels charge your batteries during the day, and you draw from stored energy in the evening, at night, and during outages. The result is a significantly higher proportion of your energy coming from your own system rather than the grid, lower monthly electricity costs, and a property that keeps running when the power goes out.

Why Battery Storage Makes Financial Sense
A solar installation without battery storage still saves money, but it has a fundamental limitation. You only benefit from the power your panels produce while you are actively using it. For most households, the peak production window is during the middle of the day when people are at work or school and electricity demand at home is at its lowest. The hours when demand is highest, typically evenings when everyone is home, are also when the panels are producing the least.

Battery storage bridges that gap. Instead of feeding surplus daytime generation back to the grid at a low feed-in tariff, you store it and use it yourself at times when it would otherwise cost you money to buy from Eskom. As electricity tariffs continue to rise, the value of every unit you store and self-consume increases alongside them.

For businesses that previously relied on diesel generators during outages, the comparison is even more direct. A battery system replaces an ongoing, unpredictable fuel cost with a fixed capital investment that pays for itself over time and requires very little ongoing maintenance by comparison.

Adding Batteries to an Existing Solar System
If you already have solar panels but no battery storage, adding batteries is often straightforward. The key factor is whether your existing inverter is compatible with battery storage. Some older inverters are grid-tied only and cannot manage a battery bank, in which case an inverter upgrade is required alongside the battery installation. We assess your existing setup and confirm what is needed before quoting.

Battery System Sizing
Getting battery capacity right is important. Too little storage and you run out before morning or during an extended outage. Too much and you are paying for capacity you never use. We size battery systems based on your actual electricity usage data, your priorities (whether that is maximum bill reduction, outage protection, or both), and your budget. We will give you clear options rather than defaulting to the largest system available.

Battery Storage: Key Considerations
Before choosing a battery system, here are the most important factors to understand:

Factor
What It Means for You
Usable capacity
The amount of energy actually available to use, expressed in kilowatt-hours (kWh). Bigger is not always better — right-sizing matters more.
Cycle life
How many charge and discharge cycles the battery is rated for before its capacity degrades significantly. More cycles means longer useful life.
Inverter compatibility
Not all batteries work with all inverters. We confirm compatibility before recommending any battery system.
Warranty
Battery warranties vary. We only install batteries with solid local warranty support so you are not left without recourse if something goes wrong.
Scalability
Can you add more batteries later if your needs grow? We design systems with future expansion in mind where possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I add batteries to my existing solar panels?
In most cases yes, but it depends on your inverter. If your current inverter is a grid-tied only model it cannot manage a battery bank and will need to be replaced or supplemented with a battery inverter. We assess your existing setup and confirm what is needed before providing a quote.

How much battery storage do I need?
This depends on how much electricity you use in the evenings and overnight, how long you want to be able to run during an outage, and your budget. We work this out from your actual usage data rather than guessing. A system that is right for your usage will always outperform an oversized one that costs more to purchase and sits partially unused.

How long do solar batteries last?
Battery lifespan is measured in charge and discharge cycles rather than years, because how heavily you use the battery affects how long it lasts. Most quality lithium batteries are rated for several thousand cycles before their capacity drops to around 80% of the original. For a system cycling once per day, that typically translates to a useful lifespan of eight to fifteen years depending on the technology and usage pattern.

Do batteries require maintenance?
Modern lithium battery systems require very little maintenance compared to older battery technologies. The main things to monitor are the battery's state of health over time and the accuracy of your inverter's charge settings. We check both as part of our annual solar maintenance visits.

What happens to the batteries during a power outage?
During an outage, the system automatically switches to battery power and your property continues running from stored energy. How long the batteries last depends on their capacity and what you are running. Essential appliances on a well-sized system can typically run through a standard load shedding cycle and into the following morning before the panels start charging again.

Is battery storage worth it if load shedding improves?
Yes. Even without load shedding as a factor, battery storage increases the proportion of your electricity that comes from your own panels rather than the grid. As Eskom tariffs continue to rise, every unit you self-consume rather than buy becomes more valuable. The financial case for battery storage is not dependent on load shedding continuing at current levels.
