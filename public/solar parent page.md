Solar Power Solutions in Somerset West, Stellenbosch and the Helderberg
Electricity in South Africa is getting more expensive every year. Eskom tariffs have increased by more than 400% over the past 15 years, and further approved increases are already in the pipeline. For homeowners and businesses that rely entirely on the grid, that trajectory has a direct and growing impact on monthly costs and long-term budgets.

Solar power changes that equation. When you generate your own electricity, every unit your panels produce is a unit you do not buy from the grid at an ever-increasing tariff. Over a system's lifespan, that saving compounds significantly. Add battery storage and you also protect yourself from outages, reduce your dependence on diesel generators, and gain a level of energy independence that becomes more valuable the less predictable the grid becomes.

The Real Cost of Depending on the Grid
Most people think about their electricity bill in terms of what they pay each month. But the more useful question is what that bill will look like in five or ten years if nothing changes. Eskom's approved tariff increases mean that grid electricity will continue to get more expensive regardless of what happens with load shedding.

Businesses face this pressure acutely. Many have turned to diesel generators as a stopgap during outages, but diesel is not cheap and running a generator through multiple load shedding cycles a day adds up fast. A commercial client running a generator through daily outages can easily spend tens of thousands of rand a year on fuel alone. Solar with battery backup eliminates that recurring cost and replaces unpredictable fuel spend with a fixed, once-off capital investment.

For homeowners, the picture is similar. A solar system that offsets most of your electricity usage locks in a large portion of your energy costs at today's price, shielding you from future increases. In the Western Cape, where sunshine hours are consistently high throughout the year, systems here produce energy reliably and the financial returns are among the strongest in the country.

Load shedding is still a reality and battery storage addresses it directly. But even if the grid became perfectly reliable tomorrow, the case for solar in the Western Cape would remain strong on financial grounds alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if solar is worth it for my property?
The starting point is your current electricity bill and how it has changed over the past few years. If your costs have been climbing and you expect them to keep climbing, solar gives you a way to lock in a significant portion of your energy costs at a fixed capital investment rather than paying an ever-increasing tariff indefinitely. The Western Cape's sunshine hours make systems here particularly effective, and a site visit gives us the information we need to give you a realistic picture of what a system would cost and what it would save over time.

What is the difference between a hybrid system and an off-grid system?
A hybrid system is connected to the municipal grid and uses solar panels and batteries to reduce your grid reliance and provide backup during outages. An off-grid system has no grid connection at all and relies entirely on solar and battery storage to meet your energy needs. Hybrid systems suit most homes and businesses. Off-grid is typically for rural or remote properties where grid connection is impractical or unavailable.

Can I add batteries to my existing solar installation?
Yes. If you already have solar panels but no battery storage, adding batteries is a straightforward upgrade in most cases. We assess your existing system, check that the inverter is compatible, and install the batteries correctly. This is one of the most common requests we receive from clients who installed solar before load shedding became as severe as it is now.

How long does a solar installation last?
Quality solar panels typically carry a 10-year product warranty and a 25-year performance guarantee. Inverters have a shorter lifespan, typically 10 to 15 years, and batteries vary depending on the technology and how heavily they are cycled. A well-maintained system should serve you reliably for 20 to 25 years.

Do you handle commercial solar installations?
Yes. We install commercial solar systems up to 100kW for offices, retail properties, guesthouses, wine estates, farms and other commercial clients. Our current project at Val De Vie Estate in Stellenbosch is an example of the scale we work at on the commercial side.

What happens if my solar system stops working?
Call us. We carry out fault diagnosis on solar systems and can identify whether the issue is with the panels, the inverter, the batteries or the electrical connections. Most faults can be diagnosed and repaired in a single visit.
