DB Board Upgrades and Repairs in Somerset West, Stellenbosch and the Helderberg
Your distribution board, or DB board, is the heart of your property's electrical system. Every circuit in your home or building runs through it. When it is working properly you never think about it. When it is not, everything stops.

A tripping board, a burning smell near the meter box, breakers that will not reset, or a board that simply cannot handle your current electrical load are all signs that something needs attention. Left too long, a faulty or outdated DB board is not just inconvenient. It is a fire risk and a safety hazard.

Is Your DB Board Causing Problems?
Most people do not know much about their DB board until it starts causing issues. The board sits quietly in a cupboard or on a wall and most homeowners never think about it until a breaker trips or something stops working. The problem is that by the time a DB board is visibly failing, it has often been struggling for a while.

Older properties in the Helderberg and Boland frequently have boards that were installed decades ago and have not been touched since. They were not designed to handle the electrical load of a modern household, let alone the addition of solar panels, a generator, air conditioning, multiple appliances and home office equipment running simultaneously.

Newer properties can also have issues. A board that was undersized for the property, fitted with cheap components, or installed without proper compliance can cause the same problems as an old one.

Our DB Board Services
We handle the full range of DB board work for residential and commercial clients:

DB Board Repairs
If a specific breaker is tripping repeatedly, a circuit has stopped working, or you have noticed unusual sounds or smells from your board, we will diagnose the fault and repair it. In many cases a repair is all that is needed, and we will tell you honestly if that is the case rather than recommending a full replacement when one is not necessary.

DB Board Upgrades
If your existing board is too small for your current load, fitted with outdated components, or needs to accommodate new circuits for solar, a generator or additional appliances, an upgrade may be the right solution. We upgrade boards to modern standards with correctly rated breakers, proper earthing and surge protection where needed.

DB Board Replacements
Some boards are beyond repair or so outdated that replacement is the safer and more cost-effective option. We remove the old board, install a new one correctly sized for your property, and make sure everything is wired, labelled and compliant before we leave.

Surge Protection Installation
Power surges from the grid, from load shedding switching events, or from lightning strikes can damage appliances and equipment connected to your circuits. Surge protection fitted at the DB board level gives your entire property a layer of protection. This is especially relevant for properties with solar systems, home offices or sensitive electronics.

COC After DB Board Work
Any DB board repair, upgrade or replacement requires a Certificate of Compliance to be issued on completion. Our registered electricians issue the COC as part of every job, so you have the correct documentation for your records, your insurer and any future property transaction.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does my DB board keep tripping?
The most common reasons are an overloaded circuit, a faulty appliance drawing too much current, a failing breaker, or a wiring fault somewhere in the circuit. The first step is to identify which breaker is tripping and whether it happens when a specific appliance is switched on. We can diagnose the cause and fix it properly.

Is a tripping DB board dangerous?
A breaker that trips occasionally as a result of a temporary overload is doing its job. A breaker that trips repeatedly, will not reset, or a board that shows signs of burning or overheating is a different matter and needs to be looked at as soon as possible. Do not ignore a burning smell or scorch marks near your board.

What is the difference between a repair and a replacement?
A repair fixes a specific fault within an otherwise serviceable board. A replacement is needed when the board itself is outdated, undersized, or damaged to the point where repairing individual components is no longer practical or safe. We assess each board on its own merits and recommend what is genuinely needed rather than defaulting to replacement.

Do I need to upgrade my DB board if I am getting solar installed?
Often yes. Solar panels and inverters add new circuits that need to be properly connected to your board. If your existing board does not have the capacity or the correct configuration for solar, an upgrade is required as part of the installation. We assess this during the initial site visit for any solar project.

How long does a DB board replacement take?
Most residential board replacements are completed in a single day. Commercial replacements depend on the size and complexity of the installation. We will give you a clear timeline before we start so you can plan around any downtime.

Will my power be off during the work?
Yes, for the duration of the work on the board your power will need to be off. For most repairs and upgrades this is a matter of a few hours. For full replacements it may be longer. We work as efficiently as possible to keep the downtime to a minimum and we will let you know in advance what to expect.

Do I get a COC after DB board work?
Yes. A Certificate of Compliance is issued on completion of every DB board repair, upgrade or replacement we carry out. This is a legal requirement for electrical work in South Africa and is important for your insurance and any future property transaction.

Can an old DB board affect my insurance?
Yes. Many insurers will not pay out on electrical-related claims if the installation is found to be non-compliant or if there is no valid COC. An outdated or faulty board that has not been inspected can leave you exposed. Getting it assessed and a COC issued puts you in a much stronger position.
